### 0. Purpose
- **Why** was this originally made, and **when**?
- **How** was it made?
- **What** is its current purpose, and **what** happened between then and now?
- **Who** controls/lives in the area?
- **Why** are the adventurers finding this place relevant?
### 1. Shape
- Define line shape of perimeters from material.
- Define number of floors and connection structure.
- Determine rough shape outline (hexagonal, circular, labyrinth, etc.). Do not think about rooms yet.
### 2. Function
- Define rooms that would exist in this context.
- Find their placement related to each other.
- Stretch, squash, and fill the rough shape.
- Add primary structures (columns, pits, stairs, subrooms and divisions)

### 2a. Puzzles
- Add specific interactables (levers, switches, platforms, ritual circles, turrets) and at least one hint to begin. The goal of the puzzle should also be obvious (open a door, get an item, stop catastrophe). Note each interactable to the players.
- Add hazards, if any (pits again, lava, weaponry, trap triggers) to either punish wrong answers or make correct ones difficult.
- Puzzles are best done in 3-5 steps. Not every step should have only one solution, but one possible route should be thought of per step.
### 3. Dressing
- Add movable objects and decorations.
- Enemy placement.
- Alter previous primary structures as needed for story or variety. 
- Items and their cash values.
